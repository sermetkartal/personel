# ADR 0024 — ISO 27001 + ISO 27701 Combined Certification Plan

**Status**: Accepted (Phase 3)
**Related**: ADR 0022 (GDPR Expansion), ADR 0023 (SOC 2 Type II)

## Context

ISO 27001:2022 certifies an Information Security Management System (ISMS) and is the default European security certification demanded in enterprise RFPs. ISO 27701:2019 extends 27001 with Privacy Information Management (PIMS) controls and is the natural pair for a GDPR-processing product.

A **combined 27001+27701 certification** is audited in a single engagement by the same certification body and carries approximately 20% additional audit effort over 27001 alone. The substantive work overlap between 27001, 27701, SOC 2 (ADR 0023), and GDPR (ADR 0022) is large: a single ISMS + PIMS suffices for all four.

Constraints:

- Phase 3 is 52 weeks. 27001 Stage 1 + Stage 2 audits typically take 8–12 weeks calendar after readiness.
- A risk assessment methodology must be selected and applied (ISO 27005 is the natural choice).
- An independent internal audit is required before the external Stage 1 audit; the compliance officer cannot audit their own ISMS.
- The certification body must offer dual 27001+27701 accreditation; some only cover 27001.
- No named certification bodies in this document.

## Decision

### ISMS scope statement

**Scope**: The ISMS covers the design, development, operation, and support of the Personel User Activity Monitoring platform (both on-prem and SaaS editions) including all personnel, facilities, processes, and technology used in its lifecycle.

**Scope exclusions**: customer-operated on-prem deployments where the customer is the data controller and system operator — Personel's ISMS covers software delivery and support, not the customer's operation.

### Annex A:2022 control mapping (abbreviated)

ISO 27001:2022 has 93 Annex A controls in 4 themes:

| Theme | Controls | Implementation anchor |
|---|---|---|
| **A.5 Organizational (37)** | Policies, roles, supplier mgmt, threat intel, classification, labelling, access, information transfer, privacy (A.5.34) | Company policies (new), sub-processor registry, DPO function, data classification policy |
| **A.6 People (8)** | Screening, employment terms, awareness, disciplinary, remote work, confidentiality | HR policies + onboarding/offboarding + security awareness training |
| **A.7 Physical (14)** | Physical perimeter, entry, monitoring, equipment, cabling, maintenance, secure disposal | Cloud provider inheritance (regional DC) + office physical security policy |
| **A.8 Technological (34)** | User endpoints, privileged access, IAM, network security, crypto, dev lifecycle, logging, vuln mgmt, backup, redundancy | Mapped 1:1 to existing ADRs (ADR 0013 crypto, ADR 0014 audit logging, ADR 0021 network, etc.) |

The Statement of Applicability (SoA) — the mandatory ISO 27001 artifact — is produced in Phase 3.3 as `docs/compliance/iso27001-soa.md`. Every Annex A control is either:
- **Applied** with an implementation reference, or
- **Excluded** with an explicit justification (expected exclusions: few; most technical controls apply).

### ISO 27701 (PIMS) additions

ISO 27701 adds controller-specific and processor-specific controls on top of 27001. For Personel as a processor (SaaS) and as a software provider (on-prem):

- **Clause 5** — PIMS-specific requirements. Extends risk assessment to privacy risks.
- **Clause 6** — PIMS-specific guidance on 27001 controls (e.g., A.5.34 privacy extended).
- **Annex A (controller-specific)** — largely not applicable to Personel-as-processor; applicable to customers.
- **Annex B (processor-specific)** — applies to Personel in SaaS mode:
  - B.8.1 customer agreement (DPA)
  - B.8.2 organization's purposes (no processing beyond instructions)
  - B.8.3 marketing and advertising (not applicable; no customer data used for marketing)
  - B.8.4 infringing instruction (refusal procedure)
  - B.8.5 customer obligations (advising customer of their obligations)
  - B.8.6 records related to processing
  - Additional processor controls on sub-processors, international transfers, deletion

The GDPR work in ADR 0022 produces most of this substrate; 27701 formalizes it into an auditable PIMS.

### Risk assessment methodology: ISO 27005

**ISO 27005:2022** is adopted as the risk assessment methodology. The risk assessment uses a scenario-based approach:

1. **Asset identification**: systems, data categories, processes, personnel.
2. **Threat identification**: by asset class (from threat model + MITRE ATT&CK for enterprise).
3. **Vulnerability identification**: from security testing, pen tests, code review, vendor advisories.
4. **Risk analysis**: likelihood × impact on 5-point scales, both based on defined rubrics.
5. **Risk evaluation**: against risk acceptance criteria (defined in the risk management policy).
6. **Risk treatment**: accept / transfer / mitigate / avoid; mapped to control implementation.
7. **Residual risk**: acceptance by management after treatment.

Risk assessment is performed annually and after any significant change (e.g., adding a new region, a major architectural change like Phase 3 SaaS launch).

### Internal audit schedule

- **Phase 3.4 (weeks 29–36)**: first full internal audit against ISMS+PIMS. Auditor: independent consultant (NOT the compliance officer). Output: findings list with remediation deadlines.
- **Phase 3.5 (weeks 37–44)**: remediation + external audit stages.

After Phase 3, internal audits are scheduled quarterly with each control area audited at least annually.

### Management review cadence

Management review meetings are held **quarterly** with the CEO, CTO, DPO, compliance officer, and head of engineering. Inputs include:
- Status of actions from previous reviews
- Changes in external/internal issues (legal landscape, threats)
- Information on ISMS/PIMS performance (incidents, non-conformities, audit results, KPIs)
- Feedback from interested parties (customers, regulators)
- Risk assessment results
- Opportunities for continual improvement

Outputs include decisions on continual improvement and resource allocation. Minutes are maintained as an ISO-required record.

### Certification body selection criteria (no named bodies)

1. **Accreditation**: must be accredited by a member of the International Accreditation Forum (IAF) for ISO 27001 and ISO 27701.
2. **Dual certification in one engagement**: must offer 27001+27701 combined audit (some bodies only accredit 27001).
3. **Presence in EU and TR**: preferably has auditors physically locatable in both regions to evaluate regional data centers (ADR 0021).
4. **Experience with cloud-native / SaaS clients**: evidenced by client references (not just industry average).
5. **Cost**: typical range for combined 27001+27701 first-certification audit for a ~50-person organization is €25k–€75k for Stage 1+2 (excluding surveillance audits in years 2–3).

Decision captured in Phase 3.0 procurement document.

### 27701 PIMS as extension of KVKK/GDPR work

The PIMS documentation burden overlaps heavily with:
- KVKK framework (existing)
- DPIA template (existing)
- VERBİS export (existing)
- GDPR DPA + Art. 30 (ADR 0022)

Estimated incremental effort for 27701 on top of 27001+GDPR work: **~20%**. This is the key efficiency argument for doing all three in Phase 3 rather than sequentially.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Combined 27001+27701 certification is a strong enterprise-sales asset, especially in EU procurement.
- Shared substrate with SOC 2 (ADR 0023) means evidence is collected once and serves three audits.
- ISO 27005 risk framework formalizes the existing risk register.

### Negative

- Management review cadence adds a recurring meeting obligation that cannot be skipped; non-attendance of the CEO breaks a required record.
- Independent internal audit requires an external consultant (compliance officer cannot audit their own work).
- 27001:2022 is still relatively new; certification body auditor familiarity varies. Auditor selection must verify 2022 vintage competence.
- Surveillance audits in years 2 and 3 create continuing cost; planning must include these.

## Alternatives Considered

- **ISO 27001 only, defer 27701**: rejected — incremental cost of 27701 is ~20%, deferring loses the efficiency and delays EU enterprise sales.
- **ISO 27701 without 27001**: rejected — 27701 requires a 27001 base.
- **BSI 10012 or other privacy standards**: rejected — less internationally recognized than 27701.
- **Map controls to NIST CSF instead**: rejected — NIST CSF is a framework, not a certifiable standard; the commercial currency in EU is ISO.
- **Self-attest on ISO 27001 without certification**: commercially weaker; rejected for Phase 3.
